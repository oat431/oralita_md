# 05.5 Past Simple

> **Formula:** S + V2

The storytelling tense — for completed actions at a specific time in the past. This is the most-used past tense in English.

---

## Structure

| | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---|------------|----------|----------|
| All subjects | I **worked** / **went**. | I **didn't work** / **didn't go**. | **Did** you work? / **Did** you go? |

⚠️ After `did`/`didn't`, the main verb returns to **V1**:
> "I **went**." → "I **didn't go**." (NOT "didn't went")
> "**Did** you **see** it?" (NOT "Did you saw?")

---

## When to Use

### 1. Completed Action at a Specific Past Time
The action is finished. The time is stated or understood.

| Example | Time Signal |
|---------|------------|
| I **went** to the market **yesterday**. | yesterday |
| She **graduated** **in 2020**. | specific year |
| They **got** married **last month**. | last + time |
| He **called** me **10 minutes ago**. | ...ago |
| I **saw** her **this morning**. (morning is over) | finished time |

### 2. Sequence of Past Actions
A series of completed actions, one after another.

| Example |
|---------|
| I **woke up**, **brushed** my teeth, **ate** breakfast, and **left** for work. |
| He **opened** the door, **walked** in, and **sat** down. |

### 3. Past Habits (No Longer True)
Repeated actions in the past that don't happen anymore.

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I **played** football every day when I was a child. | I don't anymore |
| She **smoked** for 10 years. (She quit.) | Past habit, now stopped |
| We **lived** in Chiang Mai when I was young. | We don't live there now |

> **Used to** is another way: "I **used to play** football every day."

### 4. Past Facts & States
Things that were true in the past but aren't anymore.

| Example | Now? |
|---------|------|
| He **was** a teacher. | Not anymore |
| I **had** long hair. | Not anymore |
| The company **was** much smaller then. | It's bigger now |

---

## Time Expressions for Past Simple

| Expression | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **yesterday** | I saw him yesterday. |
| **last** (night/week/month/year) | She arrived last week. |
| **...ago** | He left 5 minutes ago. |
| **in** + past year/month | I was born in 1995. |
| **when** + past event | When I was a child... |
| **this morning/afternoon** (if finished) | I ate this morning. |

---

## Regular Verbs: -ed Pronunciation

| Sound | Rule | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| /t/ | After voiceless sounds (p, k, f, s, sh, ch) | work**ed** /t/, laugh**ed** /t/ |
| /d/ | After voiced sounds (b, g, v, z, m, n, l, r, vowels) | play**ed** /d/, liv**ed** /d/ |
| /ɪd/ | After /t/ or /d/ | want**ed** /ɪd/, need**ed** /ɪd/ |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| Yesterday I go to the mall. | Yesterday I **went** to the mall. | Past time needs V2 |
| I didn't went. | I **didn't go**. | V1 after didn't |
| Did you saw it? | **Did** you **see** it? | V1 after did |
| She was go to school. | She **went** to school. | "Be" + Ving for continuous, not simple past |

> Thai uses `เมื่อวาน` + present verb. English requires the verb itself to change form.

---

## Quick Test

1. I ___ (see) a great movie last night. → **saw**
2. She ___ (not/come) to the party. → **didn't come**
3. ___ you ___ (finish) the report? → **Did / finish**
4. We ___ (live) in London for 5 years. (not anymore) → **lived**
5. He ___ (wake up), ___ (eat), and ___ (leave). → **woke up, ate, left**
