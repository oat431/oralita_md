# 05.10 Future Continuous

> **Formula:** S + will + be + Ving

The "in the middle of" future tense — for actions that will be in progress at a specific future time. It paints a picture of what will be happening.

---

## Structure

| | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---|------------|----------|----------|
| All subjects | I **will be working**. | I **won't be working**. | **Will** you be working? |

---

## When to Use

### 1. Action in Progress at a Specific Future Time
Something will be happening at an exact moment in the future. Like a snapshot.

| Example | Future Time |
|---------|------------|
| This time tomorrow, I **will be flying** to Tokyo. | Exact future moment |
| At 8pm tonight, I **will be watching** the game. | Specific time |
| Don't call at 3 — I**'ll be having** a meeting. | Reason not to interrupt |
| What **will** you **be doing** at this time next year? | Imagining future snapshot |

### 2. Action That Will Happen Naturally (Not Planned)
The action will happen as part of the normal course of events — no special intention needed.

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I**'ll be seeing** John at the meeting anyway. | It'll happen naturally |
| We**'ll be passing** a supermarket on the way. | Part of the route |
| She**'ll be arriving** around 6 as usual. | Normal routine |

> This is a polite way to talk about future actions without implying a special plan or promise.

### 3. Polite Questions (No Pressure)
Asking about someone's plans without putting pressure on them.

| Example | vs Direct Version |
|---------|------------------|
| **Will** you **be using** the car tonight? | (Neutral — just checking) |
| Are you going to use the car? | (Sounds like you want something) |
| **Will** you **be coming** to the party? | (Casual question) |
| Are you coming to the party? | (Sounds like you expect an answer) |

> Future Continuous questions feel softer and more polite than Present Continuous or "going to" questions.

---

## Future Continuous vs Future Simple

| Future Continuous | Future Simple |
|------------------|---------------|
| Action in progress at a future moment | Simple future action |
| I **will be eating** at 8pm. (in the middle of eating) | I **will eat** at 8pm. (start at 8pm) |
| She **will be working** all day. (ongoing) | She **will work** tomorrow. (simple fact) |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| This time tomorrow I will fly. | This time tomorrow I **will be flying**. | Specific future moment needs continuous |
| I will be work at 9am. | I **will be working** at 9am. | Missing -ing |
| Don't call at 8. I will eat. | Don't call at 8. I**'ll be eating**. | In progress at that time |

---

## Quick Test

1. At 10am tomorrow, I ___ (sit) in a meeting. → **will be sitting**
2. This time next week, we ___ (lie) on the beach. → **will be lying**
3. ___ you ___ (use) the computer later? → **Will / be using**
4. Don't come at 7 — I ___ (cook) dinner. → **will be cooking**
5. I ___ (pass) the bank anyway, so I can get cash. → **'ll be passing**
