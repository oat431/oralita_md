# 05.4 Present Perfect Continuous

> **Formula:** S + has/have + been + Ving

The "how long" tense — emphasizes the **duration** of an action that started in the past and is still happening now (or just stopped).

---

## Structure

| | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---|------------|----------|----------|
| I / You / We / They | I **have been working**. | I **haven't been working**. | **Have** you been working? |
| He / She / It | She **has been working**. | She **hasn't been working**. | **Has** she been working? |

---

## When to Use

### 1. Action Started in Past, Still Continuing NOW
The action began in the past and hasn't stopped. The focus is on HOW LONG.

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I **have been studying** for 3 hours. | I started 3 hours ago, I'm still studying |
| It **has been raining** all day. | Started this morning, still raining now |
| She **has been waiting** since 2pm. | Started at 2pm, still waiting now |
| How long **have** you **been learning** English? | Started in the past, continuing now |

### 2. Action Just Stopped — Result Visible Now
The action was happening until very recently. You can see the evidence.

| Example | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| You look tired. — I **have been working** all night. | Tired face = evidence |
| The ground is wet. It **has been raining**. | Wet ground = evidence |
| Her eyes are red. She **has been crying**. | Red eyes = evidence |
| I'm out of breath. I **have been running**. | Heavy breathing = evidence |

---

## Present Perfect vs Present Perfect Continuous

| | Present Perfect | Present Perfect Continuous |
|---|----------------|---------------------------|
| **Focus** | Result / completion | Duration / process |
| **Example** | I **have painted** the room. (done — result visible) | I **have been painting** the room. (focus on the activity, may not be finished) |
| **Example** | She **has written** 10 emails. (count — how many) | She **has been writing** emails all morning. (duration — how long) |
| **With "how many"** | ✅ How many pages have you read? | ❌ |
| **With "how long"** | ❌ (can use but less common) | ✅ How long have you been reading? |

---

## Verbs That Prefer Present Perfect (Not Continuous)

Some verbs are rarely used in continuous form — even for duration, use Present Perfect.

| Verb Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| State verbs | I **have known** her for 10 years. (NOT "have been knowing") |
| | She **has had** that car since 2019. (NOT "has been having") |
| | I **have been** here since 8am. (NOT "have been being") |
| Verbs of sense | I **have heard** about that. (NOT "have been hearing") |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| I work here for 5 years. | I **have been working** here for 5 years. | Duration from past → now needs this tense |
| It rains since morning. | It **has been raining** since morning. | Action continuing from past |
| She is studying for 3 hours. | She **has been studying** for 3 hours. | Present continuous can't express duration from past |
| I have been knowing her for years. | I **have known** her for years. | "Know" is stative — no continuous |

---

## Quick Test

1. I ___ (wait) for you since 5pm. → **have been waiting**
2. How long ___ you ___ (learn) English? → **have / been learning**
3. She's tired because she ___ (work) all day. → **has been working**
4. I ___ (know) him since we were kids. → **have known** (stative!)
5. The ground is wet. It ___ (rain). → **has been raining**
