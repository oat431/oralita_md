# 05.2 Present Continuous

> **Formula:** S + am/is/are + Ving

The "now" tense — for actions happening at this moment, temporary situations, and definite future plans.

---

## Structure

| | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---|------------|----------|----------|
| I | I **am working**. | I **am not working**. | **Am** I working? |
| He / She / It | She **is working**. | She **is not working**. | **Is** she working? |
| You / We / They | They **are working**. | They **are not working**. | **Are** they working? |

---

## When to Use

### 1. Actions Happening RIGHT NOW
At this exact moment — the action is in progress.

| Example | Context |
|---------|---------|
| I **am writing** a note right now. | As I speak |
| Look! It **is raining**. | Happening now |
| She **is sleeping**. Don't wake her. | Current state |

### 2. Temporary Situations
Things happening around now, but not permanently.

| Temporary | vs | Permanent |
|-----------|----|-----------|
| I **am staying** with my friend this week. | | I **live** in Bangkok. |
| She **is working** from home this month. | | She **works** at a bank. |
| He **is studying** for his exam these days. | | He **studies** engineering. |

### 3. Trends & Changes
Things that are changing or developing over time.

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| The world **is getting** warmer. | Gradual change |
| English **is becoming** more important. | Trend |
| House prices **are rising**. | Ongoing development |

### 4. Definite Future Plans
Arrangements that are already fixed — often with a time expression.

| Example | Why Present Continuous? |
|---------|------------------------|
| I **am meeting** John tomorrow at 3pm. | Arrangement is set |
| We **are flying** to Tokyo next week. | Tickets booked |
| She **is getting** married in June. | Date is fixed |
| What **are** you **doing** this weekend? | Asking about plans |

> Compare: "I **will meet** John tomorrow." (decision made now, less definite)
> vs "I **am meeting** John tomorrow." (already arranged, more definite)

### 5. Repeated Annoying Actions (with "always")
To express irritation about something that happens too often.

| Example | Tone |
|---------|------|
| He **is always complaining**! | Annoying habit |
| You **are always losing** your keys! | Frustration |
| She **is constantly checking** her phone. | Irritation |

> Compare: "He **always complains**." (neutral fact)
> vs "He **is always complaining**!" (I'm annoyed by it)

---

## ⚠️ Verbs NOT Used in Continuous (Stative Verbs)

Some verbs describe states, not actions. They stay in Present Simple.

| Category | ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| Thoughts | I am knowing... | I **know**... |
| Feelings | She is liking it. | She **likes** it. |
| Senses | Are you hearing me? | **Do** you **hear** me? |
| Possession | I am having a car. | I **have** a car. |

**Exception:** Some stative verbs can be continuous with a different meaning:

| Verb | Simple (state) | Continuous (action) |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| **think** | I **think** it's good. (opinion) | I **am thinking** about it. (considering) |
| **have** | I **have** a car. (possess) | I **am having** lunch. (eating) / I **am having** a problem. (experiencing) |
| **see** | I **see** the mountain. (sight) | I **am seeing** a doctor tomorrow. (meeting) |
| **taste** | This soup **tastes** good. (state) | The chef **is tasting** the soup. (action) |

---

## Spelling Rules for -ing

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs: +ing | work → work**ing**, eat → eat**ing** |
| Silent -e: drop e + ing | make → mak**ing**, write → writ**ing** |
| CVC (stressed): double final consonant | sit → sit**ting**, run → run**ning**, swim → swim**ming** |
| -ie: ie → ying | die → d**ying**, lie → l**ying** |

> ⚠️ "write" → "writing" (NOT "writeing"). "make" → "making" (NOT "makeing").

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| I working right now. | I **am working** right now. | Missing "be" verb |
| She is work now. | She is **working** now. | Missing -ing |
| I am knowing the answer. | I **know** the answer. | Stative verb — no continuous |
| He is always complain. | He is always **complaining**. | Missing -ing after "is always" |

---

## Quick Test

1. Look! It ___ (rain). → **is raining**
2. I ___ (stay) with my parents this week. → **am staying**
3. She ___ (not/come) to the party tonight. → **is not coming**
4. ___ you ___ (listen) to me? → **Are / listening**
5. He ___ (always/interrupt) me! → **is always interrupting**
