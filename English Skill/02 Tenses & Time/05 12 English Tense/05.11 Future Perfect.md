# 05.11 Future Perfect

> **Formula:** S + will + have + V3

The "done by then" tense — for actions that will be completed BEFORE a specific point in the future. It looks BACK from a future moment.

---

## Structure

| | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---|------------|----------|----------|
| All subjects | I **will have finished**. | I **won't have finished**. | **Will** you have finished? |

---

## When to Use

### 1. Action Completed Before a Future Time
The action will be done by a specific deadline or point in the future.

| Example | Deadline |
|---------|----------|
| By next month, I **will have finished** the project. | Next month = deadline |
| She **will have left** by the time you arrive. | Before you arrive |
| By 2030, they **will have built** the new airport. | 2030 = completion date |
| We **will have been** married for 10 years next June. | Looking back from next June |

### 2. Looking Back from a Future Point
Imagining yourself at a future moment, looking back at what's already done.

| Example | Perspective |
|---------|------------|
| By the time I retire, I **will have worked** for 40 years. | Looking back from retirement |
| In 5 years, I **will have paid off** my mortgage. | Looking back from 5 years from now |
| By Friday, you **will have received** the package. | From Friday's perspective, looking back |

---

## Common Time Expressions

| Expression | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **by** + time | **By** tomorrow, **by** next week, **by** 2026 |
| **by the time** + clause | **By the time** you arrive / **By the time** I'm 30 |
| **in** + duration (looking back) | **In** 5 years' time |
| **before** + event | **Before** the meeting starts |

---

## Future Perfect vs Future Simple

| Future Perfect | Future Simple |
|---------------|---------------|
| Emphasizes completion BEFORE a future point | Simple future action |
| I **will have finished** by 5pm. (Done before 5pm) | I **will finish** at 5pm. (Finish at 5pm) |
| She **will have graduated** by next summer. | She **will graduate** next summer. |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

This tense has no direct Thai equivalent. The instinct is to avoid it — but it's useful for deadlines and milestones.

| ❌ Avoidance | ✅ Natural English |
|-------------|-------------------|
| I will finish before you come. | I **will have finished** by the time you come. |
| Next month, I work here for 5 years. | Next month, I **will have worked** here for 5 years. |
| Before Friday, I will complete it. | By Friday, I **will have completed** it. |

---

## Quick Test

1. By the time you arrive, I ___ (finish) cooking. → **will have finished**
2. She ___ (leave) before the meeting starts. → **will have left**
3. By 2030, they ___ (complete) the bridge. → **will have completed**
4. In 2 years, we ___ (pay off) the loan. → **will have paid off**
5. ___ you ___ (read) the book by next week? → **Will / have read**
