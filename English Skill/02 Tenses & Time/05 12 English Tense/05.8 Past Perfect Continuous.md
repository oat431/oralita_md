# 05.8 Past Perfect Continuous

> **Formula:** S + had + been + Ving

The "how long before" tense — emphasizes the duration of an action that was in progress BEFORE another past action happened.

---

## Structure

| | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---|------------|----------|----------|
| All subjects | I **had been working**. | I **hadn't been working**. | **Had** you been working? |

---

## When to Use

### 1. Duration Before Another Past Action
An action was happening for a period of time, and then something else happened. The focus is on HOW LONG.

| Example | Timeline |
|---------|----------|
| I **had been waiting** for an hour when the bus finally came. | Waiting (1 hour) → bus came |
| They **had been driving** for 6 hours before they stopped. | Driving (6 hours) → stopped |
| She was exhausted because she **had been working** all day. | Working (all day) → exhausted |
| He **had been studying** English for 2 years before he moved to London. | Studying (2 years) → moved |

### 2. Past Action with Visible Past Result
The action happened before another past point, and the evidence was visible.

| Example | Evidence at Past Point |
|---------|----------------------|
| The ground was wet. It **had been raining**. | Wet ground (at that past moment) |
| Her eyes were red. She **had been crying**. | Red eyes (at that past moment) |
| He was out of breath. He **had been running**. | Heavy breathing (at that past moment) |

---

## Past Perfect vs Past Perfect Continuous

| | Past Perfect | Past Perfect Continuous |
|---|-------------|------------------------|
| **Focus** | Completion / result | Duration / process |
| **Example** | She **had painted** the room. (it was done) | She **had been painting** the room. (she was in the middle of it) |
| **Example** | He **had read** the book. (finished it) | He **had been reading** the book. (was reading, may not have finished) |
| **With "how long"** | ❌ Unnatural | ✅ How long had you been waiting? |

---

## When NOT to Use It

This is the least common of all 12 tenses in everyday speech. Don't force it.

| ❌ Overuse | ✅ Simpler (Better in Speech) |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| I had been living there for 5 years. | I **lived** there for 5 years. |
| She had been working at a bank. | She **worked** at a bank. |

> Use it only when you specifically need to emphasize duration before another past event. In most cases, Past Simple or Past Perfect is enough.

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

This tense doesn't exist conceptually in Thai. Most learners avoid it entirely — and in casual speech, that's usually fine.

| If You Want to Try It | ✅ |
|----------------------|----|
| ฉันรอมาหนึ่งชั่วโมงแล้วรถถึงมา | I **had been waiting** for an hour when the bus came. |
| เขาทำงานมาทั้งวันเลยเหนื่อย | He was tired because he **had been working** all day. |

---

## Quick Test

1. I ___ (wait) for 30 minutes when she finally arrived. → **had been waiting**
2. They were tired because they ___ (travel) all night. → **had been traveling**
3. How long ___ you ___ (study) before you took the test? → **had / been studying**
4. The grass was wet. It ___ (rain). → **had been raining**
5. She ___ (work) there for 10 years before she got promoted. → **had been working**
