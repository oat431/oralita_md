# 05.9 Future Simple

> **Formula:** S + will + V1

The "decision and prediction" tense — for spontaneous choices, promises, and forecasts. The simplest future tense to form but with important nuances.

---

## Structure

| | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---|------------|----------|----------|
| All subjects | I **will go**. | I **won't go**. | **Will** you go? |

> "Will" never changes form. Contraction: I'll, you'll, he'll, she'll, it'll, we'll, they'll.

---

## When to Use

### 1. Spontaneous Decisions (Made at the Moment of Speaking)
You decide something right now — you didn't plan it before.

| Example | Context |
|---------|---------|
| The phone is ringing. I**'ll answer** it. | Decision made NOW |
| I'm tired. I think I**'ll go** to bed. | Momentary decision |
| "Someone's at the door." — "I**'ll get** it." | Instant offer |
| Did you send the email? — Oh no, I forgot. I**'ll do** it now. | Sudden realization + decision |

> ⚠️ If it's a pre-existing plan, use Present Continuous or "going to", not "will":
> "I**'m meeting** John tomorrow." (planned) vs "I**'ll meet** John." (just decided)

### 2. Predictions (Based on Opinion, Not Evidence)
What you think or believe will happen.

| Example | Basis |
|---------|-------|
| I think it **will rain** tomorrow. | Opinion |
| She **will probably be** late. | Guess |
| I'm sure you**'ll pass** the exam. | Belief |
| One day, people **will live** on Mars. | Future forecast |

> Compare with "going to" (prediction based on evidence):
> "Look at those clouds! It **is going to rain**." (evidence — dark clouds)
> "I think it **will rain**." (opinion — no evidence)

### 3. Promises, Offers, Threats, Requests
Using "will" shows intention and commitment.

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| Promise | I **won't tell** anyone. I promise. |
| Offer | I**'ll help** you with that. |
| Threat | If you do that again, I**'ll call** the police. |
| Request | **Will** you help me? |
| Willingness | The car **won't start**. (refuses to start) |

### 4. Future Facts (Things That Will Definitely Happen)
Events that are certain regardless of anyone's plans.

| Example |
|---------|
| I **will be** 30 next year. |
| The sun **will rise** at 6:15 tomorrow. |
| Christmas **will fall** on a Thursday this year. |

---

## Will vs Going To

| | Will | Going To |
|---|------|----------|
| **Decision time** | Decided NOW | Decided BEFORE |
| **Prediction basis** | Opinion / belief | Evidence / signs |
| **Example** | I'll have coffee. (just decided) | I'm going to have coffee. (already planned) |
| **Example** | I think it'll rain. (opinion) | Look! It's going to rain. (dark clouds) |

---

## Shall (Formal / British)

"Shall" is used in formal British English for offers and suggestions with I/we.

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **Shall** I open the window? | Offer — "Do you want me to...?" |
| **Shall** we dance? | Suggestion — "Let's..." |
| What **shall** we do tonight? | Asking for suggestion |

> In American and modern English, "will" replaces "shall" in nearly all cases. "Shall" survives mainly in "Shall we...?" and legal documents.

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| I will go tomorrow. (when it's a plan) | I**'m going** tomorrow. / I**'m going to go** tomorrow. | Use will only for spontaneous decisions |
| It will rain. Look at the clouds! | It**'s going to rain**. Look! | Evidence → going to |
| I promise I come. | I promise I**'ll come**. | Promise needs will |
| Tomorrow I will went. | Tomorrow I **will go**. | V1 after will, never V2 |

---

## Quick Test

1. The phone is ringing. I ___ (answer) it. → **'ll answer**
2. Look at those clouds! It ___ (rain). → **'s going to rain** (evidence!)
3. I promise I ___ (not/be) late. → **won't be**
4. ___ you ___ (help) me with this? → **Will / help**
5. I think she ___ (get) the job. → **will get**
