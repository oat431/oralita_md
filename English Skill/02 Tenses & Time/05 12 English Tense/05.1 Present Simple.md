# 05.1 Present Simple

> **Formula:** S + V1(s/es)

The simplest tense — but the most versatile. Used for facts, habits, and timeless truths.

---

## Structure

| | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---|------------|----------|----------|
| I / You / We / They | I **work**. | I **don't work**. | **Do** you work? |
| He / She / It | She **works**. | She **doesn't work**. | **Does** she work? |

---

## When to Use

### 1. Facts & General Truths
Things that are always true — scientific facts, universal statements.

| Example | Type |
|---------|------|
| Water **boils** at 100°C. | Scientific fact |
| The sun **rises** in the east. | Universal truth |
| Bangkok **is** the capital of Thailand. | Fact |

### 2. Habits & Routines
Repeated actions — daily, weekly, regularly. Often with frequency adverbs.

| Frequency | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| always (100%) | I **always drink** coffee in the morning. |
| usually | She **usually takes** the bus. |
| often | They **often eat** out on Fridays. |
| sometimes | We **sometimes walk** to work. |
| rarely / seldom | He **rarely watches** TV. |
| never (0%) | I **never eat** meat. |

### 3. Scheduled Events (Future Meaning)
Timetables, schedules, programs — things on a fixed calendar.

| Example | Context |
|---------|---------|
| The train **leaves** at 6pm. | Timetable |
| The meeting **starts** at 9am tomorrow. | Schedule |
| My flight **arrives** at midnight. | Fixed plan |

### 4. Instructions & Directions
Giving steps, recipes, or directions.

| Example |
|---------|
| First, you **mix** the flour and sugar. |
| You **turn** left at the traffic light, then **go** straight. |

### 5. Commentary / Narration
Live sports commentary, storytelling for dramatic effect.

| Example |
|---------|
| Ronaldo **passes** to Messi... Messi **shoots**... and he **scores**! |

### 6. Verbs of Thinking & Feeling (Stative Verbs)
Some verbs rarely use continuous form — they describe states, not actions.

| Category | Verbs |
|----------|-------|
| Thoughts | know, believe, understand, remember, think (opinion), suppose |
| Feelings | like, love, hate, prefer, want, need |
| Senses | see, hear, smell, taste, seem |
| Possession | have, own, belong |

> ❌ "I am knowing the answer." ✅ "I **know** the answer."
> ❌ "She is wanting coffee." ✅ "She **wants** coffee."

---

## Spelling Rules for -s/-es

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Most verbs: +s | work → work**s**, eat → eat**s** |
| -s, -ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -o: +es | watch → watch**es**, go → go**es**, miss → miss**es** |
| Consonant + y: y → ies | study → stud**ies**, cry → cr**ies** |
| Vowel + y: +s | play → play**s**, buy → buy**s** |
| have → has | She **has** a car. |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| He work at a bank. | He **works** at a bank. | Missing -s for he/she/it |
| She don't like coffee. | She **doesn't like** coffee. | Wrong auxiliary |
| Does she likes it? | **Does** she **like** it? | Main verb returns to V1 after does |
| I always am late. | I **am always** late. | Frequency adverb before main verb, after "be" |

---

## Quick Test

1. The sun ___ (rise) in the east. → **rises**
2. She ___ (not/eat) meat. → **doesn't eat**
3. ___ you ___ (speak) English? → **Do / speak**
4. The train ___ (leave) at 8pm tonight. → **leaves**
5. He ___ (have) two brothers. → **has**
