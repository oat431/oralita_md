# 05.7 Past Perfect

> **Formula:** S + had + V3

The "past of the past" — for an action that happened BEFORE another past action. It establishes the sequence when the order matters.

---

## Structure

| | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---|------------|----------|----------|
| All subjects | I **had finished**. | I **hadn't finished**. | **Had** you finished? |

> "Had" is the same for all subjects. No -s, no irregular forms for the auxiliary.

---

## When to Use

### 1. Action Before Another Past Action
Two past events — the earlier one uses Past Perfect, the later one uses Past Simple.

| Example | Sequence |
|---------|----------|
| When I arrived, the movie **had already started**. | Movie started → I arrived |
| She **had finished** her homework before she went out. | Homework done → went out |
| They **had eaten** dinner by the time we got home. | They ate → we got home |
| I didn't recognize him because he **had changed** so much. | He changed → I didn't recognize |

> The Past Perfect tells you which action came first without needing to explain the sequence.

### 2. "Already" and "Just" with Past Perfect
Emphasizing that something happened earlier or only moments before.

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I **had already left** when he called. | Earlier action — I was gone |
| She **had just finished** when the boss arrived. | Moments before |
| He **had never seen** snow before he moved to Canada. | No experience before that point |

### 3. Reported Speech (Backshift from Past Simple / Present Perfect)
When reporting what someone said, Present Perfect and Past Simple shift to Past Perfect.

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|--------------|-----------------|
| "I **have finished**." | She said she **had finished**. |
| "I **went** to Paris." | He said he **had gone** to Paris. |
| "I **never saw** it." | She said she **had never seen** it. |

### 4. Third Conditional (Unreal Past)
Expressing regret about something that didn't happen.

| Example | Reality |
|---------|---------|
| If I **had studied**, I would have passed. | I didn't study |
| If she **had told** me, I would have helped. | She didn't tell me |
| I wish I **had known** earlier. | I didn't know |

---

## When NOT to Use Past Perfect

Don't use Past Perfect just because something is past. Use it ONLY when you need to show that one past action happened before another.

| ❌ Overuse | ✅ Simple Past (Better) |
|-----------|------------------------|
| I had woken up, had brushed my teeth, and had eaten breakfast. | I **woke up**, **brushed** my teeth, and **ate** breakfast. |
| He had been a teacher. (no second past action to compare to) | He **was** a teacher. |

> **Rule:** If you're just listing past events in order, use Past Simple. Use Past Perfect only when you need to jump BACKWARD in time relative to another past event.

---

## Past Perfect vs Past Simple

| Sentence | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| When I arrived, she **left**. | I arrived, then she left. (sequence: arrive → leave) |
| When I arrived, she **had left**. | She left before I arrived. (sequence: leave → arrive) |

> Same words, different tense = completely different meaning.

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai expresses sequence through context words (ก่อน, หลังจาก, แล้ว), not verb forms. Past Perfect feels redundant.

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| When I arrived, the movie started already. | When I arrived, the movie **had already started**. |
| She finished before I came. (OK casual) | She **had finished** before I came. |
| I didn't know he changed so much. | I didn't know he **had changed** so much. |

---

## Quick Test

1. The concert ___ (already/start) when we arrived. → **had already started**
2. She ___ (never/see) snow before she moved to Canada. → **had never seen**
3. I didn't go because I ___ (see) the movie before. → **had seen**
4. ___ you ___ (finish) the report before the meeting? → **Had / finished**
5. He said he ___ (lose) his phone. → **had lost**
