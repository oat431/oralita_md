# 05.3 Present Perfect

> **Formula:** S + has/have + V3

The "bridge" tense — connects the past to the present. It's about the **present result** or **relevance** of a past action, not the past action itself.

---

## Structure

| | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---|------------|----------|----------|
| I / You / We / They | I **have finished**. | I **haven't finished**. | **Have** you finished? |
| He / She / It | She **has finished**. | She **hasn't finished**. | **Has** she finished? |

---

## When to Use

### 1. Past Action with Present Result
Something happened in the past — the result matters NOW.

| Example | Present Result |
|---------|---------------|
| I **have lost** my keys. | I can't open the door now. |
| She **has broken** her leg. | She can't walk now. |
| He **has eaten** too much. | He feels sick now. |
| They **have painted** the room. | The room looks different now. |

### 2. Life Experience (Ever / Never)
Talking about things that have (or haven't) happened in your life — time is NOT specified.

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I **have visited** Japan three times. | In my life, so far |
| She **has never eaten** sushi. | Zero experience in her life |
| **Have** you ever **been** to Europe? | At any point in your life |
| He **has climbed** Mount Everest. | Life achievement |

> ⚠️ If you specify WHEN → use Past Simple, not Present Perfect.
> "I **have been** to Japan." ✅ (experience, no specific time)
> "I **went** to Japan **last year**." ✅ (specific time — use Past Simple)
> "I have been to Japan last year." ❌

### 3. Unfinished Time (Today, This Week, This Year)
The time period is still continuing — more can happen.

| Example | Why Present Perfect? |
|---------|---------------------|
| I **have drunk** three coffees today. | Today isn't over yet |
| She **has called** me twice this morning. | This morning is still going |
| We **have had** a busy week. | The week isn't finished |
| He **hasn't eaten** anything since breakfast. | The period continues to now |

> Compare: "I **drank** three coffees yesterday." (yesterday = finished time → Past Simple)

### 4. Just, Already, Yet

| Word | Position | Meaning | Example |
|------|----------|---------|---------|
| **just** | Before V3 | Very recently (a moment ago) | I've **just** arrived. |
| **already** | Before V3 | Sooner than expected | She's **already** finished. |
| **yet** | End of sentence (negative/question) | Expected to happen | I haven't finished **yet**. / Have you finished **yet**? |

### 5. Since & For (Duration)

| Word | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **since** | Starting point (a moment in time) | I've lived here **since** 2020. / **since** I was a child. |
| **for** | Duration (length of time) | I've lived here **for** 4 years. |

> ⚠️ "I am living here since 2020." ❌ → "I **have lived** here since 2020." ✅

---

## Present Perfect vs Past Simple

| Present Perfect | Past Simple |
|----------------|-------------|
| Unspecified time / present relevance | Specific time in the past |
| I **have seen** that movie. (experience) | I **saw** that movie **yesterday**. |
| She **has gone** to Japan. (she's still there) | She **went** to Japan **last month**. (she came back) |

### Gone vs Been

| Form | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **have gone** | Went and still there | She **has gone** to Japan. (She's in Japan now) |
| **have been** | Went and came back | She **has been** to Japan. (She went and returned) |

---

## ⚠️ Thai Speaker Traps

Thai uses `แล้ว` (láew) for completed actions — learners often map this to Present Perfect when Past Simple is correct.

| ❌ Error | ✅ Correct | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| I have eaten breakfast this morning. | I **ate** breakfast this morning. (if morning is over) | Past Simple for finished time |
| I have seen him yesterday. | I **saw** him yesterday. | Specific past time → Past Simple |
| I am here since 2015. | I **have been** here since 2015. | Duration with since → Present Perfect |
| She has went home. | She **has gone** home. | Wrong V3 form — went → gone |
| I have ever been there. | I **have been** there. | "Ever" only in questions/negatives in this position |

---

## Quick Test

1. I ___ (lose) my phone. I can't find it. → **have lost**
2. She ___ (work) here since 2018. → **has worked**
3. ___ you ever ___ (eat) Thai food? → **Have / eaten**
4. He ___ (not/finish) his homework yet. → **hasn't finished**
5. I ___ (see) that movie yesterday. → **saw** (Past Simple — specific time!)
